## In plain language

The rain that the river management board forecast yesterday has arrived: today's 3:44 PM update confirms precipitation is falling in the central basin today and tomorrow, and the board's message is unchanged — the recession is expected to slow to a halt over the next few days before gradually resuming later next week. The overnight drop was about 4 centimetres (midnight to mid-afternoon), consistent with a pace that is already beginning to taper.

Hydro-Québec's dam at the foot of Lac Coulonge (Bryson) held a steady posture today — releases barely changed, the dam pond is sitting quietly within its normal operating range. The more notable operational news comes from the basin's terminal dam (Carillon, near Grenville): its total release was cut nearly 10% since yesterday — roughly 456 cubic metres per second less — which appears to be a deliberate step-down coinciding with today's rain event, likely to manage downstream water levels near Ottawa and Montreal. Despite the cut, the Carillon headpond continued to drift lower. A further upstream change worth noting: the Première-Chute dam, further up the Gatineau-side tributary cascade, shifted its mix of water considerably — cutting turbine output by about 25% while increasing spillway flow by the same amount, without much change in total release. This is a posture change that often signals operators expecting higher inflows ahead.

At Carillon, the headpond is now 35 centimetres above its spring regulatory ceiling for the ninth consecutive day. The ceiling (under the integrated watershed management plan) is binding whenever the gauge at Hull is above 42.61 metres — which it remains, by 40 centimetres. The good news is the overshoot has declined from a peak of 44 cm on May 10 to 35 cm today, with most of that improvement in the past 24 hours. Rain and operator responses over the next day or two will determine whether that trend continues.

Bottom line for property owners: the lake is still dropping, but the pace will likely stall within the next day or two because of this rain event. At the current rate, exit from minor flood (107.50 m threshold) is roughly 6 days away in an optimistic scenario (rain brief, recession resumes quickly); 10–15 days if the halt extends. The northern basin, meanwhile, shows its first small uptick at Mattawa in several days — a reminder that more snowmelt water is still working its way south. Watch for tomorrow's ORRPB update (4:00 PM EDT) for the revised trajectory.
