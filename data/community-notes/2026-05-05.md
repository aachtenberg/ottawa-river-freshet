## 1. Dan Poole — reservoir narrative (received 2026-05-05)

> May 5, 2026 — Reservoir Status
>
> Most reservoirs are now reaching the 80–85% full range as runoff continues
> across the northern basin. Under normal conditions, peak operating levels

> manage potential precipitation events.
>
> In the upper system, storage is actively being built at Dozois and Quinze.

### Validation against cluster data

Source: `latest_reservoir_readings` and `reservoir_readings` (May-3 vs May-4
daily snapshots; ORRPB scrape, 21:30 UTC ingest).

**Rising / falling / stable count.** Dan reports 11 rising, 1 falling,
1 stable. Cluster shows:

| Bucket | Reservoirs (24 h Δ in cm) |
|---|---|
| Rising (≥ +1 cm) | baskatong (+8), cabonga (+1), dozois (+12), kiamika (+1), kipawa (+3), lady_evelyn (+2), mitchinamecus (+6), poisson_blanc (+5), quinze (+2), rapide_7 (+1), timiskaming (+1) — **11** |
| Falling | des_joachims (−2) — **1** |
| Stable | bark_lake (0), timiskaming_haileybury (0) — **2** |

Match on rising / falling counts. Dan reports 1 stable; cluster shows 2.
Likely Dan collapses bark_lake (Madawaska / OPG, off the Ottawa main stem)
out of his count.

**"Cabonga and Baskatong leading in capturing inflows."** If "leading" is read
as *24 h rate*, this is mostly the wrong call: top rate-gainers in cluster
data are **Dozois (+12 cm)**, **Baskatong (+8)**, **Mitchinamecus (+6)**,
**Poisson Blanc (+5)**. Cabonga is +1 cm — flat. If "leading" is read as
*absolute storage volume captured* (Cabonga is one of the basin's largest
reservoirs by surface area), the framing is plausible but unverifiable from
levels alone — would need volumetric tables. We don't have those.

**"80–85% full."** Cannot validate from cluster data. The cluster does not
ingest operator-published high/low operating limits per reservoir. Dan's
calculation method (linear interpolation across the operating range) is
sound; we just don't have the limits cached. Worth a future ingester
addition.

**"Gatineau River appears to be reaching its peak."** Cluster data is
consistent:

- Baskatong (upper Gatineau headwater): **+8 cm / 24 h** → upper basin still
  capturing inflow.
- Paugan (HQ 3-65, lower Gatineau, m³/s): **−0.85 / 24 h, −16.4 / 72 h** →
  mild decline; lower system already coming off.

Pattern matches Dan's reading: storage still building above; outflow at the
mouth easing.

**Kiamika gauge restored.** Cluster confirms — Kiamika reporting again as
of 2026-05-04 with a +1 cm 24 h Δ. Prior to restoration the row would have
been stale or missing.

ORRPB slopes computed from May-5 → May-8 nominal endpoints (3-day window).
Cluster slopes from latest reading vs same hour 24 h / 72 h prior.

**Headline observation.** Cluster-observed declines at the lower-basin
gauges (Carillon, Hull, Britannia) are running **roughly 1.5× to 3×
faster** than ORRPB's 4-day forecast slopes implied. Upper-basin (Mattawa)
is also declining faster on a 24 h basis but reverts toward the forecast
slope on a 72 h average. ORRPB forecasts are typically conservative
(operators prefer to over-forecast levels rather than under-forecast in
flood season), so this divergence is not surprising — but it suggests
actual conditions on May 8 are likely to come in **below** the ORRPB
endpoints, particularly at Carillon (potentially ~6 200 m³/s rather than
6 350 if the current rate holds).

**Caveat.** Cluster slopes are point-in-time-to-point-in-time. Daily
operations (turbine schedules, spill changes) introduce diurnal noise —
the 72 h slope is the better long-trend signal. The headline still holds
on the 72 h figures: Carillon 72 h rate is −141/d, Hull is −8.2 cm/d, both
faster than ORRPB's 3-day implied slopes (−50/d and −5 cm/d).
