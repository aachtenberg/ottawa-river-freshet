# Carillon Impounded Water Management Plan — Operating Envelope (October 2004)

**Source:** Page 6 of *Impounded Water Management Plan Summary, Carillon Project, October 2004*. Circulated in the Ottawa River flood-watch community files (provided by community member 2026-05-04).

**Provenance:** This is the operational-constraints page of Hydro-Québec's Carillon WMP as of October 2004. The document is referenced in Ontario MNRF correspondence (2021–2023, archived privately) as the antecedent of the LRIA-approved Carillon Impounded Management Plan. It establishes the *seasonal operating envelope* of the Carillon headpond — directly relevant to the case-file argument that current operations exceed the binding flood-period ceiling.

## The operating envelope (verbatim transcription, page 6, Table 2.1)

> Below are the operating constraints in normal and flood periods with regard to the safety of persons and property immediately up and downstream of the Carillon dam. They discuss the general and specific constraints for the Carillon powerhouse forebay for managing impacts upstream of the project. They also cover the specific constraints involving the flows downstream of the project, which make it possible to manage the impacts immediately downstream.
>
> **2.1 General Constraints Associated with Forebay Management**
>
> The general constraints associated with management of the Carillon powerhouse forebay define the maximum and minimum levels to be respected in regular operations as well as the critical maximum and minimum levels to avoid at all times. The following table gives their values.

| Upstream level (m) | Value | Comments (verbatim) |
|---|---|---|
| Critical maximum | 41.5 | As per agreement. |
| **Operating maximum** | **41.15** | "As per agreement. During the spring flood, the maximum level is 40.08 m when the level at the Hull dock exceeds the servitude level of 42.61 m." |
| **Operating minimum** | **39.62** | "As per agreement. Outside the boating season, the minimum operating level is 40.54 m. During boating season, the minimum is 40.84 m. **During the flood period, the minimum is 39.62 m.**" |
| Critical minimum | 39.62 | — |

**Marginal note in the source PDF:** *"what Agreement?"* — handwritten annotation flagging that the source document does not specify which "agreement" the table references (likely a separate Carillon-specific agreement with Ontario or the 1983 Ottawa River Regulation Agreement; would be worth establishing definitively if the case file is escalated).

## How to read the table

The Carillon headpond has **four operating envelopes**, depending on season and basin condition:

| Period | Operating min (m) | Operating max (m) | Range (m) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Spring flood (Hull dock > 42.61 m) | **39.62** | **40.08** | **0.46** |
| Outside boating season (fall/winter, normal flow) | 40.54 | 41.15 | 0.61 |
| Boating season (summer, normal flow) | 40.84 | 41.15 | 0.31 |
| Critical bounds (avoid at all times) | 39.62 | 41.50 | — |

The **spring-flood envelope is the case-file-relevant one.** It is triggered when the level at the Hull dock exceeds the easement servitude level of 42.61 m (the same threshold cited in Ontario expropriation easement RR19542B). Within this envelope, the headpond must be operated between 39.62 m and 40.08 m — a 46 cm band, not a 1.53 m band as the general operating range would suggest.

## Direct comparison to live observations (May 2026)

The dashboard's live telemetry shows Carillon amont (Hydro-Québec station 1-2968) holding at approximately **40.49 m** through April–May 2026 (12-day band 40.43–40.54 m, see Phase 6 / Phase 7 sections of the case-file summary).

| Bound | Value (m) | Current (m) | Δ |
|---|---|---|---|
| Spring-flood operating maximum | 40.08 | 40.49 | **+41 cm above ceiling** |
| Spring-flood operating minimum | 39.62 | 40.49 | +87 cm above floor |
| Outside-boating-season operating minimum | 40.54 | 40.49 | -5 cm (within band) |

If the Hull dock has exceeded the servitude level of 42.61 m at any point during the 2026 freshet (a condition that should be straightforwardly verifiable against the Hull gauge), then Carillon was operating outside its published spring-flood envelope by approximately 41 cm. The previously-asserted "88 cm above the directive low" framing in the case file was directionally correct but understated — the more accurate framing is that the actual current level is *above the published flood maximum*, not just above the published flood minimum.

## What this evidence means for the case file

1. **The published operating envelope is much tighter than previously stated.** During spring flood, the band is 39.62–40.08 m (46 cm), not 39.62–41.15 m (1.53 m). The case-file's existing language understates the constraint.

2. **Current observed Carillon amont exceeds the flood-period ceiling**, not just the flood-period floor. This is a more pointed claim and easier to falsify or confirm: if Hull dock has been > 42.61 m at any point this freshet, the WMP says Carillon must be ≤ 40.08 m, and the dashboard data says it has been ~40.49 m for the entire 12 days observed.

3. **The constraint is published in the operator's own document.** It is not interpretation, not directive-versus-practice, not "what should be done." It is the published operating maximum during spring flood as stated in HQ's own Impounded Water Management Plan Summary. The framework-exists / enforcement-doesn't argument now has its strongest single piece of evidence.

4. **Verifying Hull dock > 42.61 m for 2026** is the next step. The case file's existing data sources should make this checkable: Vigilance has Hull-area stations, and the case-file dashboard tracks the Gatineau Rapides-Farmers gauge. Worth confirming the trigger condition formally.
