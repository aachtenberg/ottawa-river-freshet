EXHIBIT G · THE SELECTED WINDOW · ORRPB FRAMING TEST Northern Reservoirs Flood Watch · Compiled May 7 2026

The 50-year record is real.
It's also the only window where it is.

On May 6 the Ottawa River Regulation Planning Board told CBC News that 2026 set a 54-year basin precipitation record between March 1 and April 15. The claim is true at that exact window — and false in every standard climatological window that frames it. The 2025–26 fall-winter was the second-driest on record. The full pre-freshet season ranked dead middle. 2026 wasn't a wet year; it was a normal year on a compressed schedule. The framing chose the only window in which the record exists.

The claim under test
"This spring, the board said a preliminary assessment of precipitation received between March 1 and April 15 shows the total amount was the highest recorded in the last 50 years."
— Ottawa River Regulation Planning Board, statement to CBC News, published 2026-05-06

What this means
2026 wasn't a wet year. It was a record snowfall on a frozen basin, melted on a compressed schedule.
Two findings stack. First, every station shows the same cherry-pick pattern: 2026's spring window held roughly double the share of seasonal total compared to historical norms; the fall-winter season was the second-driest on record; total pre-freshet water input (Oct 1 – Apr 15) was within the normal range — rank 27 of 54. Second, that "record precipitation" was almost entirely snow. 7 of 8 stations averaged sub-zero across the window, with means as cold as −7.7 °C and 27–38 sub-zero days out of 46. Only Ottawa CDA in the south averaged above freezing. At every station that actually drives Lac Coulonge inflow, the precipitation fell as snow on already-frozen ground — augmenting the snowpack, not running into the river. It didn't enter the freshet flow until mid-April warming finally broke the freeze. Reaching major flood under those conditions is diagnostic of the operational response — not the precipitation.
Bottom line
The ORRPB's claim is technically true and rhetorically misleading. They said "precipitation" against a 6-week window without saying rain, without saying where, without saying that at sub-zero temperatures the precipitation joined the snowpack. 2026 was a dry fall-winter followed by record late snowfall onto a frozen basin, all melted on a compressed schedule when warming finally arrived. The simplest explanation that fits all observations: operators executed a historical-average drawdown rule against a year that didn't match historical averages, with no mechanism to adapt. That's the case file's policy ask, made by the data.
