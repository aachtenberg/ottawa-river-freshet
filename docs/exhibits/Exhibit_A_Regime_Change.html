EXHIBIT A · LAC COULONGE · YEARLY PEAK LEVELS · 1972–2026 Northern Reservoirs Flood Watch · Compiled April 2026

In 10 years, the Ottawa River
flooded as often as it did in 45.

Lac Coulonge crossed 108.5 m once between 1972 and 2016 — a forty-five-year span. It has crossed that level four times in the ten years since. The pattern is too abrupt to be climate alone. The Planning Board's official document treats it as cyclical natural variation.

What the data shows
"From 2017 to 2026, a span of 10 years versus 45 years for 1972 to 2016, we have experienced 300% more super-floods for Lac Coulonge. This is not a gradual trend upwards due to climate change. It's a major shift in management regime."
— Contributor A, Northern Reservoirs Flood Watch · 28 April 2026
What the Planning Board says
"Flooding is NOT expected to happen every second year. Flooding is driven by weather, and weather can be cyclical. Then flooding can be cyclical too. Flood events can be clustered."
— ORRPB, "Flow Management in the Ottawa River Basin," Slide 17 · April 2026
