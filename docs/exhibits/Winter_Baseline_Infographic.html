What followed
Lac Coulonge peaked at 108.63 m on April 20 — the fourth-highest peak in the modern era. The 2026 winter baseline was approximately 14 cm above the recent-decade average. The 2026 freshet peak was approximately 14 cm above the average of the four super-flood years.
— Source: ORRPB and Quebec MSP Vigilance gauge data (station 1195)

Why this matters · Winter baseline cannot be explained by snowmelt or spring rain — neither has occurred yet. It reflects how much water is being held in the upstream reservoirs and run-of-river headponds before the freshet starts. A higher pre-freshet baseline means less buffer to absorb spring runoff and a higher peak under any given snowmelt scenario. The shift visible here is an operational signal, not a weather signal.
